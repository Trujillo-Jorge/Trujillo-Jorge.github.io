Conditional statements

Java, like all other programming languages, is equipped with specific statements that allow us to check a condition and execute certain parts of code depending on whether the condition is true or false. Such statements are called conditional, and are a form of composite statement. In Java, there are two forms of conditional statements:
  • The if-else statement, to choose between two alternatives;
  • The switch statement, to choose between multiple alternatives.
